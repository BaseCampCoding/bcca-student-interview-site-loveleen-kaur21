INTRODUCTION


Hey there! I am Loveleen Kaur, a student of class of 2021 at BCCA. BCCA (Base Camp Coding Academy) is a non-profitable private organization dedicated to training high school graduates into Software Developers by the end of its 11-month course.

To give a little more insight on what BCCA is actually like, I interviewed another student at BCCA: Scott Gregory Bowles. This interview took place on November 19, 2020.






What has been the toughest week? What has been the easiest week? What is your biggest victory so far?
"The toughest week was definitely the first Unit project. I was no where near prepared enough to do all of the work we had done, and required." "The easiest week was this week with HTML and CSS, I understand everything we have been doing and is quite easy." "Getting through the first unit project. I wasn't really the most confident that i was gonna make it through the week and was expecting to get taken out of the program."



In my perspective, BCCA is a promising opportunity. We are 11 weeks in and I have already learned more than I could imagine. I started off not knowing anything about code. Yet, here I am learning different languages and making programs.

Greg leaves us off with his final thoughts: "I am enjoying Base Camp a lot. I have made plenty of friends and am enjoying working with everyone here. I would highly recommend this program to anyone who is eligible and wants to become a software developer."
